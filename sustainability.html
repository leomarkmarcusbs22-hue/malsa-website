Supply Chain Traceability

MALSA achieves 100% traceability to plantation level across all FFB sources. This transparency ensures responsible sourcing and allows our downstream partners — including IOI Corporation and Mewah Group — to verify the origin of every tonne of palm oil.

Our traceability processes are subject to independent assessment, most recently verified by Mewah Group in their public TTP (Traceability to Plantation) report. This commitment goes beyond compliance — it reflects our belief that transparency is the foundation of a trustworthy supply chain.

Environmental Responsibility

We are committed to minimising our environmental impact through responsible estate management, waste management, and compliance with Malaysian environmental regulations. Our operations are oriented toward sustainable production volumes while maintaining the ecological balance of our surroundings.

Key environmental practices include proper management of Palm Oil Mill Effluent (POME), responsible use of fertilisers and agrochemicals, erosion prevention on estate slopes, and conservation of riparian zones and water catchment areas within our concessions.

Community Development

As a significant employer in the Labuk-Sugut and Beluran districts, MALSA contributes to local economic development through employment opportunities, fair dealings with smallholders and dealers, and community engagement initiatives.

We provide housing quarters for estate workers, maintain safety and health standards across all operations, and ensure fair wages and working conditions in line with Malaysian labour regulations.

Our relationship with the local smallholder community is particularly important. By offering accessible FFB purchasing at our three collecting sites, we provide smallholders with reliable market access and fair pricing, contributing to their economic sustainability.
